# THNKBYD Brand Style Guide

## Voice

THNKBYD speaks like a premium documentary narrator — cinematic, psychological, and curiosity-driven. Every script should feel like a short film about the human mind, not a lecture or a listicle.

## Writing Style

- Cinematic pacing with vivid, concrete imagery
- Psychological depth — explore the *why* behind behaviour
- Curiosity-driven — make the viewer need to know what happens next
- Storytelling first — anchor ideas in narrative, metaphor, or real-world tension
- Emotionally engaging without being sentimental
- Premium documentary narration tone
- Minimal fluff — every sentence earns its place
- Thought-provoking — challenge assumptions, don't confirm them
- Concise paragraphs — 2–4 sentences maximum
- Powerful hook within the first 15 seconds of spoken delivery
- Smooth transitions between sections — no jarring topic jumps
- End with reflection — leave the viewer thinking, not just informed

## Avoid

- Clickbait phrasing ("You won't believe...", "This one trick...")
- AI-sounding language ("In today's fast-paced world...", "Let's dive in...")
- Generic motivation ("Believe in yourself", "Never give up")
- Repetition of the same point in different words
- Emojis

## Audience

**Age:** 18–35

**Interests:**

- Psychology
- Business
- Discipline
- Money
- Mindset
- Self-improvement

## Tone Reference

Think: high-production YouTube documentaries on human behaviour, decision-making, and the psychology of success — intelligent, restrained, and cinematic.
